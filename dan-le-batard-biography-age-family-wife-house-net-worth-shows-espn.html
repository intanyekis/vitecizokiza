Dan Le Batard Biography

Dan Le Batard is an American newspaper sportswriter, radio host, and television reporter based in Miami, Florida. He also collaborated with ESPN and the Miami Herald, for which he wrote from 1990 to 2016, for his hometown paper.

Le Batard hosts a daily radio show with Jon Weiner, which originates in Miami from WAXY and is broadcast on ESPN Radio nationally. He also hosts Highly Questionable, which airs daily on ESPN and was originally called Highly Questionable by Dan Le Batard; the program is co-hosted by Le Batard with his father Gonzalo and a revolving co-host. He is also a frequent contributor to many ESPN shows, acting as the main host of substitution for Forgive the Interruption. He is also called Baby Hippo.

Dan Le Batard Family

He was born to Cuban exile parents Gonzalo and Lourdes. They moved to Central Islip, New York, before settling in Miramar, Florida. Dan ‘s brother is artist David Le Batard, who is based in Miami and is professionally known as LEBO. Le Batard also speaks Spanish fluently, which he learned through the heritage of his parents. Le Batard spoke on his radio show during the 2016 exhibition MLB game in Cuba, about how his family risked everything to come to the U.S.

Dan Le Batard Wife

He is married to his longterm girlfriend Valerie Scheide. On September 13, 2018, Miami Herald reporter and long-time acquaintance of Le Batard’s, Greg “Scoops” Cote, published an article revealing that Dan is engaged with his two-year-old daughter, Valerie Scheide. The article was released without the consent of the couple, causing great embarrassment for Le Batard as he was on the air at the time the news broke. The couple married in November 2019 at a ceremony in Miami.

Dan Le Batard ESPN

Le Batard is a frequent contributor to many ESPN TV Network programs. He is a recurrent guest on Outside the Lines, The Sports Reporters, and College GameDay among others. He is also frequent Forgive the Interruption guest host, where he was christened “The Hateable Dan Le Batard” because of his often divisive views, as well as his unorthodox attire. Hosting duties on PTI have allowed Le Batard to implement the catch-phrase “Bam!,” which he exclaims in different ways at the beginning of every show he hosts. Le Batard has a joking rivalry with Tony Reali, former PTI stat-boy.

Dan Le Batard Miami Herald

Le Batard started working at the Miami Herald in 1990 and is a columnist for his section on sports. It was believed that his first major work for The Herald was an investigation into the football team in his former college.

Dan Le Batard Radio show

Le Batard also hosts a morning radio show weekdays with Jon “Stugotz” Weiner on ESPN Radio, in addition to his work for the Miami Herald. Le Batard is known for his self-deprecating humor, which through running jokes brings onto the show.
