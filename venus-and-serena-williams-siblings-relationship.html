What Venus And Serena's Other Siblings Really Think Of Their Success

 Posted on July 5, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  913 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Serena and Venus Williams are two of the most iconic female tennis players ever played the sport. They are a great inspiration to aspiring young tennis player aspirants who want to make it to the open tournaments but struggle financially, like how Serena and Venus used to be. With numerous companies reaching out to have brad deals with them, they have gained an unmounted amount of fame and success that they have worked for in decades. However, despite their popularity, little is known about the people always behind their back to support them, their siblings.

How many other siblings do Venus and Serena Williams have? Is one of their siblings jealous of their sisters' success? Are Venus and Serena only close to each other, or do they also have a close relationship with their siblings? Keep reading to find out...

6 How Many Siblings Did Serena Williams Have?

Serena Williams has four other sisters, with two of them being her half-siblings. Serena Williams is the youngest, and Venus is the eldest child of King Richard and Oracene Price. Their half-siblings are Lyndrea, Yetunde, and Isha Price.

King Richard made all his children try the sport tennis, which all of them were obliged to do. However, when he saw that only Venus and Serena were the ones who had the passion and an immediate connection with the sport, he let his other children pursue their respective passions outside the sport.

5 Do Venus and Serena Williams Have The Same Parents?

Venus and Serena are biological siblings and the children of two tennis players. Oracene, their mother, was a tennis coach who also personally coached Serena Williams when Serena did not have professional coaching help yet when she was young. Meanwhile, King Richard was a tennis player who worked as Venus and Serena's everyday coach, life mentor, and manager.

Their father worked hard to find the best coach for Venus and Serena, who would agree with his conditions about his girls' future. Luckily, he discovered Rick Macci, who was known to coach the best tennis players of their time, such as Jennifer Capriati.

4 The Film King Richard Was About Venus And Serena's Father

In September 2021, after more than a year of filming because of the Covid-19 delay, the film King Richard which is about Serena and Venus' father's life, was premiered. Will Smith made sure that Venus and Serena were part of the film's production as much as they could, which meant they would see through filming and give necessary details as executive producers to Will, who played King Richard.

However, despite the fantastic reviews of the movie, an author still criticized how the biopic was told as she believes the film focused more on Venus and Serena's life. However, the film was still named after the legendary Venus and Serena's father instead of them.

3 Venus And Serena's Sisters Keep Them Humble

In an Instagram post by Serena Williams with a picture of her together with her sisters, she captions, "I [Serena Willians] love this photo because we [her sisters] have a close bond. This [her sisters] is what keeps me humble. They [Serena's sisters] are not afraid to tell me anything. After all, I am the youngest of five."

Also featured in the King Richard film, King Richard himself was serious in teaching the girls the importance of humility despite all their success.

2 Isha Price Knew Venus And Serena Were Going To Be Successful

In an Interview Isha had with The Telegraph, she said, "Us older girls knew that Venus and Serena [Williams] were going to be successful from when they were nine or ten because they [Venus and Serena] enjoyed it [playing tennis] so much. We just believed it would happen, even though tennis wasn't a part of the community we grew up in."

Venus and Serena's sisters also watched out for them, especially when receiving hateful comments from people telling them they weren't enough. To help them have a positive mindset, their sisters would ask them not to read newspapers to avoid adding more pressure.

1 Serena Williams Was Jealous Of Venus Growing Up

Aside from Venus' early access to professional coaches, exposure to tennis tournaments, and media attention, Serena was also jealous of her older sister's physique.

She admitted she even had body issues growing up as she compared herself to her 'beautiful' sister, who is only a year older than her. More jealous of her beauty and less of her sister's success, Serena Williams has learned to love herself after decades of being in the tennis limelight.
