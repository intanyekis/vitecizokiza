Kevin Karlson obituary: Prominent Boston DJ and WZLX host dies at 59

 Posted on August 10, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  330 words  |  Larita Shotwell

The world of radio broadcasting mourns the loss of a true icon as Kevin Karlson, the vibrant and beloved DJ of Boston’s WZLX, bids farewell. His sudden death at the age of 59 has left a void in the industry, a void that may never truly be filled. Karlson, with his infectious humor and timeless love for classic rock, entertained listeners for nearly two decades, becoming a staple in the lives of many.

Karlson’s journey in radio began over thirty years ago, a testament to his enduring passion for the medium. His partnership with Pete McKenzie took them across various radio stations, navigating the challenges and triumphs of the industry together. Their camaraderie and comedic rapport resonated not just over the airwaves but also in the hearts of their devoted audience.

More than just a DJ, Karlson was a relatable figure, known for his amusing grumbles and his candid struggles, including his ongoing battle with weight loss. Despite not being showered with numerous awards, he earned the coveted title of ‘Employee of the Month’ in June 1994, a recognition of his dedication and significant contributions.

One of Karlson’s most significant achievements was co-hosting the “Karlson, McKenzie, and Heather” morning show on Boston’s 100.7 WZLX-FM. This 14-year-long venture became a beloved fixture in Boston, capturing the essence of the city’s spirit during morning commutes. Their blend of humor, classic rock tunes, and engaging conversations endeared them to the hearts of Bostonians, solidifying Karlson’s status as a legendary figure in classic rock radio.

Beyond his entertaining persona, Karlson was revered for his profound knowledge of classic rock, making him a trusted voice for music enthusiasts and radio aficionados alike. Although his physical presence may have departed, his legacy lives on in the hearts of those who tuned in faithfully to the iconic radio host. Kevin Karlson’s voice might be silenced, but the echoes of his laughter and the tunes he played will forever resonate in the world of classic rock radio.
