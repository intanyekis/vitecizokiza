Announcers For Auburn Tigers Football

Announcers For Auburn Tigers Football are Andy Burcham, Jason Campbell, Ronnie Brown, Paul Ellen, and Will Herring.

Andy Burcham

Sportscaster Andy Burcham is well recognized for his work covering Auburn Tigers football.

He is very close to Auburn University and is well-known in the sports broadcasting industry. Andy is one of the Auburn Football Announcers and it's common to refer to Andy Burcham as the "Voice of the Auburn Tigers."

He has a lengthy history of calling plays-by-play and doing radio commentary for Auburn University's basketball and football games.

When renowned veteran Auburn play-by-play broadcaster Rod Bramblett and his wife, Paula Bramblett, tragically died in a vehicle accident in 2019, he accepted a significant role as the primary announcer for Auburn football and men's basketball.

Jason Campbell

Jason Campbell was an Auburn University football player for the Auburn Tigers. He had a great collegiate career as an Auburn Tigers outstanding quarterback.

Leading Auburn to an undefeated season in 2004, he was a finalist for the Heisman Trophy, given to the top college football player.

The Washington Redskins picked Campbell in the first round (25th overall) of the 2005 NFL Draft. From 2005 to 2009, he was a member of the Redskins.

Campbell played for several NFL clubs, including the Oakland Raiders, Chicago Bears, Cleveland Browns, and Cincinnati Bengals, in addition to the Washington Redskins. He hung his boots from his playing career in 2015.

Ronnie Brown

Running back Ronnie Brown was a member of Auburn University's football team, the Auburn Tigers.

The Miami Dolphins selected Ronnie Brown as the second overall pick when he entered the 2005 NFL Draft. His NFL career officially began with his selection.

Ronnie Brown played professionally for several NFL teams after his tenure with the Dolphins, including the Philadelphia Eagles, San Diego Chargers, and Houston Texans. He retired from sports in 2014. During his successful NFL career, Brown was selected to the 2008 Pro Bowl while playing for the Dolphins.

During his time in the NFL, he gained over 5300 yards through rushes and scored multiple touchdowns.

Will Herring

Will Herring played for the Auburn Tigers in the University and he used to play as a linebacker. He was even named to the All-SEC team.

He was a top player for the Auburn Tigers. After hanging up his boots from his playing career, he then worked as an analyst and started working for Auburn Media.

He actually played as a Safety at the beginning of his professional career but then changed his position to Linebacker where he managed to perform beautifully and much better than when he played as a Safety.

After a successful University time with the Auburn Tigers, he was then selected by the Seattle Seahawks through the NFL draft in the fifth round of 2007. He played for multiple teams in the NFL, such as Seawaks from 2007 to 2010, and went to the New Orleans Saints but left them in 2013 to join the St. Louis Rams.

However, he was most of the time the deputy linebacker but when the team needed him, he always gave his best performance and was always professional.
